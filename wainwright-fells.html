The Wainwright Fells

Alfred Wainwright included 214 of the Lake District Fells in his guide books, now fondly known as the Wainwrights. The fells are grouped into 7 different areas, as per the seven seperate books in the series, and cover the wide range of landscapes and altitudes found within The Lake District.

Looking after the Fells

Part of the Lake District National Park and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, this spectacular landscape has many special qualities. You will find a rich cultural heritage, complex geology and geomorphology and a wealth of habitats and wildlife.

Thousands of people now enjoy fell walking through this landscape. However, their passage takes it's toll, eroding the landscape, disturbing the flora and fauna and polluting the environment. But, with a few changes you can lessen your impact:

Leave nothing behind. Take All your litter home, even those biodegradable apples cores (they are not natural to a high fells environment).
Where there is a clear path, please stick to it. Your footfalls can erode the landscape and prevent/stunt the growth of plants.
Be respectful and considerate to any plant or animal life that you encounter. Quietly observe and enjoy, taking care not to disturb or damage.
Be respectful and considerate to those living in, working in and enjoying the countryside. Be nice, say hello, share the space and leave gates and property as you find them.
Please keep any dogs under close control. Sheep and other animals can be easily worried and disturbed.
Don't light any fires or barbeques.
